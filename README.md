# COMM1190---Data-Insights-and-Decision
This is a group analytics project focused on **employee attrition at Globex Pharma**, the goal was to use **predictive analytics** to identify the main factors driving employee turnover and help the company design more effective **employee retention strategies.**

The analysis was conducted using** R in RStudio**. Our workflow included **data preparation, splitting the dataset into training and testing sets, and evaluating model performance** to ensure reliable predictions.

We developed two predictive models. The first model used **logistic regression** to estimate the probability of employee attrition based on variables such as overtime workload, job involvement, environment satisfaction, and years since last promotion. The second model used a **classification tree model (rpart)** to identify patterns and decision rules that highlight groups of employees with higher attrition risk.

Model performance was evaluated using a **confusion matrix**, which allowed us to assess metrics such as **accuracy, sensitivity, and specificity**. These evaluation steps helped determine how effectively the models could identify employees at risk of leaving.

The analysis showed that **overtime workload** and **low job involvement** were key drivers of attrition, and certain employee groups were more vulnerable when these factors occurred together. Based on these findings, the project provided recommendations to help the company improve employee engagement, review overtime practices, and strengthen career development pathways to reduce turnover.

The project also considered **ethical implications** of predictive analytics using an ethical decision-making framework, and the team followed an **Agile-style workflow** to iteratively develop and refine the analysis.
